How to Get Your Kid's Camp Schedule Into Your Family Calendar (Automatically)

Every summer, millions of parents receive a camp schedule in one of the following forms:

  • A PDF attached to an email
  • A link to a Google Sheet
  • A printout handed to the kid on the first day

And then, because life is busy and there are three kids and two parents with different work schedules, the schedule gets copied — imperfectly, partially — into someone's calendar. Or it doesn't, and you find out about the field trip at 7:45 AM when your kid mentions it while eating breakfast.

What Is an iCal Feed?

An iCal feed (also called an iCalendar subscription or .ics link) is a web address that any calendar app can subscribe to. When the organization updates their calendar — adds a makeup day, changes a pickup time, cancels an activity — your calendar app automatically picks up the change.

Think of it like subscribing to a newsletter, except instead of articles arriving in your inbox, schedule updates arrive in your calendar — automatically, in real time, without anyone having to remember to forward you anything.

Most modern camp management platforms (CampMinder, Bunk1, CampBrain, Sawyer) and sports scheduling systems (SportsEngine, TeamSnap, GameChanger) publish iCal feeds from their parent portals. The feed already exists. You just need the link.

Step 1: Find Your Camp's iCal Link

Log into your camp's parent portal and look for any of these:

  • A "Subscribe to Calendar" button or link
  • An "Add to Calendar" option near the schedule
  • A "Download .ics" or "iCal" link in the schedule view
  • The words "Subscribe," "Sync," or "Export" near the calendar section

If you don't see it immediately, try:

  1. Looking in the schedule or calendar section of the parent portal (not the home screen)
  2. Checking the camp's help docs or FAQ for "iCal" or "Google Calendar"
  3. Emailing the camp director directly: "Do you have an iCal subscription link for parents?" — most know what this means and will send it in 24 hours

Youth sports: SportsEngine, TeamSnap, and GameChanger all publish iCal feeds from the team or club page. Look for a calendar icon or "Export" option when viewing the team schedule.

Adding to Google Calendar

  1. Open Google Calendar on a computer (the subscription feature isn't available from the mobile app)
  2. In the left sidebar, find "Other calendars" and click the + button
  3. Select "From URL"
  4. Paste your camp's iCal link
  5. Click "Add calendar"

Google Calendar will now check the feed periodically (roughly every 24 hours) and add any new events automatically. The camp's events will appear in a distinct color that you can customize.

Note: Google Calendar updates subscribed calendars every 8–24 hours. For real-time updates, use an app that polls more frequently — or use Kinetic (see below), which checks all connected feeds fresh every morning before your briefing is sent.

Adding to Apple Calendar

  1. Open Calendar on your Mac
  2. Go to File → New Calendar Subscription
  3. Paste the iCal link and click Subscribe
  4. Set a name, color, and refresh frequency (every hour is fine)
  5. Make sure "Copy events to calendar: iCloud" is enabled if you want it on your iPhone automatically

On iPhone: Go to Settings → Calendar → Accounts → Add Account → Other → Add Subscribed Calendar, then paste the iCal URL.

What If My Camp Doesn't Have an iCal Feed?

Some smaller or more traditional camps still operate with emailed PDFs and paper schedules. A few options:

Ask them to enable it

If the camp uses CampMinder, Bunk1, Sawyer, or any modern camp management software, iCal feeds almost certainly exist — the feature just may not be advertised. A quick email to the director asking "do you have an iCal subscription link for the parent portal?" can unlock it.

Use Kinetic's manual entry as a fallback

Even without an iCal feed, you can add the camp's schedule events manually through Kinetic. The daily briefing will include them alongside all your other connected calendars, with weather and departure context. You only enter it once — Kinetic handles the daily delivery.

Create your own iCal feed

If you're technically comfortable, you can create a Google Calendar for the camp's schedule and share the iCal link from that calendar with anyone who wants it. Enter the schedule once, and everyone who subscribes gets the same updates.

The Two-Minute Setup That Saves You All Summer

Most families spend 15–30 minutes across the summer manually entering or re-entering camp events — plus dozens of moments of uncertainty about what's happening and when. An iCal subscription takes two minutes to set up and eliminates all of that.
