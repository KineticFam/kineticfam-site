The Best Cozi Alternatives in 2026 (For Families Who Are Done with the Ads)

Kinetic Blog · June 2026 · 5 min read
Home › Blog › The Best Cozi Alternatives in 2026 (For Families Who Are Done with the Ads)

Cozi has been around since 2005 and it shows. It was the gold standard for shared family calendars for a decade, and plenty of families still love it. But if you've been using it lately, you've probably noticed the same things everyone else is noticing:

  • The free tier is increasingly ad-heavy
  • Cozi Gold costs $39.99/year for features that should be basic
  • The interface feels dated compared to newer apps
  • It still doesn't pull schedules from camps, sports leagues, or school districts automatically

You're not imagining it. Here are the best Cozi alternatives worth switching to in 2026, with honest pros and cons for each.

  • Kinetic Family — Best for daily briefings and camp/sports families
  • SuperDuper — Best if inbox-parsing is the main problem
  • TimeTree — Best for couples and co-parenting schedules
  • Google Calendar — Best if you just need the basics
  • What to look for in a Cozi replacement

1. Kinetic Family — Best for Families in Sports and Summer Camp Season

Best for: Families managing 2+ kids across camps, sports, and school with schedules in multiple systems

Free tier: ✅ Yes  |  Platforms: Web, iOS, Android (briefing via email)

Kinetic takes an entirely different approach from Cozi. Instead of being a place where you store your schedule, Kinetic connects to the iCal feeds published by your kids' organizations — camps, soccer clubs, school districts, swim leagues — and emails your family a personalized morning briefing every day at 6:30 AM.

You wake up, open your email, and already know: what's happening today, what's tomorrow, what the weather is, and roughly when you need to leave. No app to open. No calendar to check. Just clarity in your inbox before the day starts.

The iCal advantage: Most Cozi users spend significant time manually entering events that already exist in their camp's or club's scheduling system. Kinetic eliminates that entirely — you paste one link from the camp's parent portal, and every schedule update they publish shows up in your briefing automatically.

What Kinetic does better than Cozi:

  • Daily proactive email vs. "remember to open the app"
  • Live integration with org iCal feeds — no manual entry from camp PDFs
  • Weather + departure context built into the morning briefing
  • No ads, no paywalled basic features

What Kinetic doesn't do (yet):

  • Shared shopping / grocery lists (Cozi has these)
  • In-app task management and to-do lists
  • Real-time push notifications for last-minute changes

Bottom line: If your main Cozi frustration is "I need to know what's happening with the kids today without digging through three apps," Kinetic solves exactly that. Get started free at kineticfam.com →

2. SuperDuper — Best for Gmail Families Tired of Manual Entry

Best for: Gmail households who want inbox-to-calendar automation

Free tier: ✅ Yes  |  Platforms: iOS, Android

SuperDuper reads your Gmail inbox and automatically extracts events — school dates, practice times, permission slip deadlines — and adds them to a shared family calendar. The setup is minimal and it works remarkably well for events that flow through email.

Cozi comparison: SuperDuper eliminates the manual data entry that Cozi requires. The calendar fills itself in from your inbox. Two-way Google Calendar sync is also included free (Cozi's sync is one-way on the free tier).

Limitations: Weekly summary email only (Sunday) — not daily. Gmail only for now. Can't connect to camp or club iCal feeds directly. No weather context.

Best for: Families whose main Cozi pain point is typing in events that were already in an email. If you want daily actionable briefings, pair with Kinetic.

3. TimeTree — Best for Co-Parents and Dual-Income Couples

Best for: Shared calendars between two parents who need real-time visibility

Free tier: ✅ Yes (with ads)  |  Platforms: iOS, Android, Web

TimeTree is a lean, clean shared calendar app built around simplicity. You create a calendar, invite your partner or co-parent, and both have real-time visibility into each other's schedules. It's polished, fast, and free (with tasteful ads on the free tier).

Cozi comparison: TimeTree is cleaner and faster than Cozi but less feature-rich. No shopping lists, no agenda email. Where it shines is in the basic shared calendar experience — especially for co-parenting situations where two households need visibility into the kids' schedule without a lot of complexity.

Common complaint: "Too many calendars to manage" — when you have multiple kids, multiple organizations, and multiple parent calendars, TimeTree's flat structure can get cluttered. No native iCal org integration.

4. Google Calendar — The Free Baseline

Best for: Families already deep in Google Workspace who want zero extra cost

Free tier: ✅ Always free  |  Platforms: iOS, Android, Web

If you're a Google household, Google Calendar can do most of what Cozi does — shared family calendars, color-coded events, email reminders — for free and with better integration into Gmail and Google Meet.

The honest limitation: Google Calendar is infrastructure. It doesn't know you're a parent. It doesn't send you a morning briefing. It doesn't pull your camp's schedule in automatically unless you manually find and subscribe to the iCal feed yourself. It's powerful and free but entirely passive — it waits for you to come to it.

Many families use Google Calendar as the backbone and Kinetic as the daily delivery layer on top.

What to Look for in a Cozi Replacement

Before you switch, it's worth being specific about what you actually want. Most families switching from Cozi fall into one of three buckets:

If your main frustration is ads and paywalling

→ Try TimeTree (cleaner, free) or Google Calendar (always free, no upsells)

If your main frustration is manual event entry

→ Try SuperDuper (Gmail inbox parsing) or Kinetic (iCal feed integration)

If your main frustration is "I never know what's happening until I open the app"

→ Try Kinetic — the daily morning briefing means the schedule comes to you, every day, in your inbox.

The Bottom Line

Cozi isn't broken. It's just 20 years old in a world that's moved on. The families who stay love its familiarity. The families who leave are usually chasing one of two things: less friction (no ads, no paywalls, no manual entry) or more proactivity (tell me what's happening, don't make me check).

If you want the proactive experience — an app that briefs you every morning before the day starts — Kinetic is worth 5 minutes of your time. You can have your first briefing in your inbox by tomorrow morning.
